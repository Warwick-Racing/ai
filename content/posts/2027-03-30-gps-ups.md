## Mowar Powaaaarr!

The UPS for the sensor plate has just arrived.
This is part of the teams push to improve localisation and position tracking to assist in our lap counting.

As with plenty of other teams, WRAI uses GPS to provide a first stage approximation of the vehicle position on track.
This then feeds into the SLAM system to provide our precise position but some of our fallback strategies do rely on the raw GPS position.

From testing the team knows that our GPS/IMU takes anything up to 15 minutes to stabilise and start producing it's best positional results, although it is normally faster in open environments such as Silverstone.
The major issue we have is that position lock is lost every time we power cycle the compute platform (the GPS/IMU is usb powered) and we don't always have 15 minutes between putting the sensor plate on the vehicle and beginning our run.

The team's proposed solution is to keep the GPS powered up regardless of the computer platform's state. 
A small UPS will inject power into the GPS/IMU link continuously and recharge from the vehicle when possible.

The team did look at keeping the entire sensor and compute platform powered from a UPS but based on space calculations for the required battery capacity decided not to.
